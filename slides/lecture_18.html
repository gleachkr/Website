The Puzzle of External Goods

If virtue is all that matters, how can there be good choices?

Solution

Axia

Choiceworthiness

Proegmena

Preferred or “Promoted” indifferents

Virtue is the good. But we don’t choose things because they’re part of our good. We choose things because they’re in accordance with nature.

These things possess axia—they’re worthy of choice. There are also things that possess the opposite of this, and are worthy of rejection

But axia isn’t the same as real desirability. The thing that’s unconditionally desirable is choice in accordance with nature. Virtue is like dancing, or playing music, and unlike making a ship. The activity itself is the product, not what’s left behind by the activity.

Some things with sufficient axia are to be “preferred”

Even choices are among the indifferents—it’s choices coming out of virtue that are to be preferred, rather than just “appropriate acts”
