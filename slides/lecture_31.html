Goal

Find something that you can know, even if you are being deceived by an evil demon.

An Archimedian Point.

The Cogito Argument

Version one:

Suppose I am deceived. It follows that I exist.

Version two:

Necessarily, The proposition “I exist” is true whenever it is thought.

Version three:

“When someone says ’I am thinking, therefore I am, or I exist”, he does not deduce something from thought by means of a syllogism, but recognizes it as self-evident by a simple intuition of the mind." (Descartes, Replies)

I Am. What Am I?

Conceptual Analysis

When I say “I exist”, what does this mean? What am I affirming?

  1. A rational animal exists?
  2. A body, animated by an Aristotelian soul exists?
  3. A thing that thinks exists?

Does this mean I am an immaterial thing?

Now, it is plain I am not the assemblage of members called the human body… But it is true, perhaps, that those very things which I suppose to be non-existent, because they are unknown to me, are not in truth different from myself who I know.

What is thought?

To doubt. To understand, to conceive, to affirm, to deny, to will, to refuse. To imagine, to perceive.

Or anyway, to seem to perceive.

Do we now know what we are, with greater clarity than what we thought we knew before we began?

I cannot help believing that corporeal things, whose images are formed by thought [which fall under the senses], and are examined by the same, are known with much greater distinctness…

The Mind Alone Perceives.

My mind is apt to wander, and will not yet submit to be restrained within the limits of truth. Let us therefore leave the mind to itself once more, … in order that having afterward withdrawn it… it may then be the more easily controlled

The Wax

Let us … consider the objects that are commonly thought to be the most easily and likewise the most distinctly known.

Piece of beeswax. What is it, that we perceive whenever we perceive this beeswax?

  • Not a thing with a certain level of hardness (why?)
  • Not a thing with a certain taste, a certain smell, a certain color, a certain temperature, a certain shape…

Something extended, flexible, movable.

what is meant by flexible and movable? Is it not that I imagine that the piece of wax, being round, is capable of becoming square…?

Assuredly such is not the case, because I conceive that it admits of an infinity of similar changes; and I am, moreover, unable to compass this infinity by imagination

consequently this conception which I have of the wax is not the product of the faculty of imagination

Similarly for extension.

I must, therefore, admit that I cannot even comprehend by imagination what the piece of wax is, and that it is mind alone which perceives it.

The contents of perception

I am almost led into error by the terms of ordinary language. We say, for example, that we see the same wax when it is before us… whence I should be forthwith be disposed to conclude that the wax is known by the act of sight.

But, when people pass on the street, completely bundled up, we still say we “see” the people…

Perception requires cognition

when I distinguish the wax from its exterior forms… I cannot, nevertheless, thus apprehend it without possessing a human mind.

If I judge that the wax exists because I see it, it assuredly follows much more evidently that I myself am or exist.

bodies themselves are not properly perceived by the senses, nor by the faculty of the imagination, but by the intellect alone; and since they are not perceived because they are seen or touched, but only because they are understood, I readily discover that there is nothing more easily or clearly apprehended than my own mind.
