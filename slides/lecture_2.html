Sparta

Lycurgus

  • Semi-mythical figure. Rose to power in Sparta during period of unrest.

Period of unrest following rule of Suse and aquisition of Helots. Plutarch’s Story about Thales, and Lycurgus studying government in Crete, about him traveling to Egypt, while trying not to be thought eager to become king by killing his nephew.

Spartan State

  • Citizenship requires heredity, but not automatic.

Hence, increasing problems with Helots. Hence, perpetual militarization.

  • Male agoge, beginning at 7. Dining Hall at 20. Family at 30.

  • Laws to discourage wealth (division of property, syssitia dining, prohibition on trade and craft, iron currency)

currency quenched in vinegar—fragile and intrinsically worthless.

Athens

Democracy, Persian Wars

  • Solon (democracy), Peisistreids (tyrants 546-510), Cleisthenes (democracy)

  • Ionianian Revolt (499)

  • Persian Invasions. Marathon, Thermopylae, Salamis. Athenians emerge as leaders of the Delian League.

In Athens, the persian emissaries were put on trial and executed. In Sparta, thrown down a well.

Pericles, Aspasia

  • Elected leader of Athens, 445-429BC.

  • Husband of Aspasia, friend of Protagoras, Anaxagoras, Zeno.

  • Populist (laws favoring Athenian poor)

Second Peloponesian War Begins

  • Gradual consolidation of power in Athens. Inner wall.

Transfer of Delian League treasury to Athens, misappropriation to build parthenon, etc.

  • Frivolous Embargo against Megaria leads to Spartan ultimatum.

  • War

Second Peloponesian War Ends

  • 430 Pericles evacuates Attica to Athens. Funeral Oration.

  • 431 Plague. 429, Kills Pericles.

Death of perhaps half the population of Athens.

Aftermath

  • Invasion of Melos, 416

Trojan women, Euripedes, Thucydidies

  • Total defeat, by starvation and siege, 404

  • Rule of thirty Tyrants. Restoration of Democracy in 403.

During the reign of the tyrants, one twentieth of the population of Athens was executed. Critias. Insidious—tried to implicate others in their crimes.

Socrates’ Involvement

  • Fought under Pericles

  • Refused to carry out executions under the tyrants

  • Was a friend and a teacher of Critias, Alcibiades

Plato’s Involvement

  • Born a few years before the plague.

  • Nephew of one of the tyrants Charmides, more distant relation of Critias

  • Friend of Socrates.
